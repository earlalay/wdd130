Client Report

How did you decide what content and images to use for your site plan?

I thought about websites that I had previously wished existed, and decided to make one of those. In the end, I decided to plan a website that allows you to filter different forms of media by various preferences. I thought about what people might want to avoid in the media they consume, and made those the filters. As for the photos I used, I just looked up images that directly correlated with the different types of media. And I combined those 3 - movies, songs, and books - in the logo.

How did you communicate with your developer? Were there any issues with communication?

After I had finished all the prep, including the wireframes, I sent Hyrum the link to the Word document via email. We also exchanged phone numbers. We used email for links and files, and texting for questions. There weren't really any issues with communicating. I'm pretty sure English is Hyrum's second language, so some things just took longer to explain.

Did your developer meet your expectations for your site?

He did. I really like how it came out, especially visually. He used the colors I gave him really well and did the formatting in the way I laid out.

What will you do differently next time you hire a team to do a project for you?

I wasn't 100% ready when it came time to swap wireframes and such. Next time, I'll be more prepared with what I would like. I'm sure I also could have given more information in the document, because people aren't mind readers. And some things that seem obvious to me probably aren't to other people.

Developer Report

How did you communicate with your client? Were there any issues?

Like I said above, we communicated via email and texting. There weren't any issues in communication. Sometimes I would struggle to understand what he was saying, because I don't think English is his first language. But we always got there eventually.

How did you handle publishing your client's site and sharing it with the client?

We used GitHub and made a repository with all the relevant files for the project. We shared the links to each other's website so we could see how they were coming along and give feedback.

How was your experience with managing your site with GitHub?

It was confusing honestly. I haven't really used GitHub before. I know that it is a powerful tool, and I was able to do the basic tasks needed for this project. But I didn't understand why I needed to do certain things. But I'm sure I'll get the hang of it the more I use it.

General Experience and Reflections

What was the most challenging part of this project for you? Were you able to overcome it?

I think the most challenging part of this project for me was resizing all of the images. There's probaby a way in CSS to have different sized images take up the same amount of space. But I cropped the images and used free resizers online to get them to match each other. There was a lot of trial and error there. I did overcome it. There was no part of the project that was truly challenging.

What did you enjoy most about this project?

I really liked seeing things come together near the end of the project. Especially when I added in the colors. Doing the project helped me feel capable in using the skills I learned this semester.

What would you do differently if you could do the project again?

I spent a lot of time in resizing images, and I could have done that more efficiently. I would have given more information to Hyrum up front so he had all the information he needed.

Do you feel more confident in working on real-world projects after this experience? Explain.

I do feel more confident than I was at the start of the semester. But there is still a lot I don't know, and I often have to look things up. I would be scared about making something that someone actually wanted to use long term. I don't know how to code for things to be easily updateable in the future. I'm also not good at coding it so that it works well on multiple screen sizes. So if it is a really basic site, I feel okay about doing that.
